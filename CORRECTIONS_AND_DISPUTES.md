# Corrections and disputes

Registry classifications are reviewable evidence, not ownership, identity, trademark, endorsement,
or username-availability decisions. A correction/dispute should identify the public creator/handle,
the contested fact, stable public evidence, requested correction, and any safety urgency. Do not
submit private identity documents, credentials, account-control tokens, or personal data through a
public issue or the public suggestion form.

An administrator records the request through the private review workflow, preserves provenance and
the prior decision, and applies least-public handling while investigating. Disputed/suppressed
evidence stays out of public profile output. A fuzzy, alias, separator, or confusable match is only
evidence. Critical handle/profile changes and releases retain different-person approval,
stale/replay protection, and audit logging even for urgent corrections.

If a published decision creates immediate impersonation risk, consuming platforms should use their
own manual-review/critical-name fallback; Registry downtime or `not_listed` is never permission to
grant a suspicious handle. Resolution records the evidence, reviewer, reason, timestamps, affected
release, notification decision, and follow-up. Audit history is not deleted.
